### Property Tax Detail

- **Homeowner rate (primary, 200+ days/year):** $5.95 per $1,000 assessed value
- **Residential rate (non-primary):** $11.10 per $1,000 — this applies for 3 mo/yr use
- Homeowner exemption ($50K) only available for primary residence

### HOA Fee Breakdown

| Condo Type | Monthly Range |
|------------|--------------|
| Basic/older condos | $300-$600 |
| Mid-range (pool, landscaping) | $600-$1,000 |
| Resort condos (Waikoloa, Mauna Lani) | $1,000-$2,500 |

HOA fees rising sharply due to insurance crisis — many associations doubled or tripled insurance components.

### Insurance Detail

- Standard HO-6 condo policy (no wind): $400-$1,150/year
- Hurricane coverage: adds $1,500-$3,500/year
- Hurricane deductibles: 1-5% of insured value ($6K-$30K out of pocket on $600K)
- Building-level hurricane premiums passed through in HOA fees

### Utility Costs

| Utility | Monthly Cost |
|---------|-------------|
| Electricity (~$0.40/kWh, 2.2x national avg) | $160-$450 |
| Water | $50-$70 |
| Internet | $60-$80 |
| **Total** | **$270-$600** |

### Cost Risks

- **Special assessments:** $5K-$50K (unpredictable, increasingly common)
- **HOA fee escalation:** Rising due to insurance crisis
- **Vacancy issues:** Property sits empty 9 months/year
- **Hurricane exposure:** Deductibles alone could be $6K-$30K

### Potential Offsets

- Short-term rental income when not using (check HOA rules — many restrict)
- Property appreciation (currently flat/declining on Big Island)

## Option B: Vacation 3 Months/Year (Long-Term Rental)

### Rental Costs (30+ Day Monthly Rates)

| Area | Monthly Range |
|------|-------------|
| Kailua-Kona | $2,500-$5,000 |
| Waikoloa | $3,000-$6,000 |
| Hilo | $1,500-$3,000 |
| Captain Cook / South Kona | $2,000-$3,500 |

Best platforms: VRBO, Furnished Finder, Craigslist Hawaii, Facebook groups, direct with property managers. 90-day direct bookings yield 30-50% off nightly rates.

### Annual Cost for a Couple (3 Months, Kona Area)

| Expense | Low | Mid | High |
|---------|-----|-----|------|
| Rental (90-day rate, 1BR condo) | $7,500 | $12,000 | $18,000 |
| Airfare (2 people RT) | $1,000 | $1,800 | $3,000 |
| Car rental (3 months) | $2,400 | $3,600 | $5,000 |
| Food/groceries | $3,600 | $5,400 | $7,200 |
| Gas + misc | $1,000 | $2,000 | $3,500 |
| **Total annual** | **$15,500** | **$24,800** | **$36,700** |

### Cost-Saving Tips

- **Car:** Local agencies (Harper Car & Truck Rental) or Turo: $800-$1,500/mo vs $1,200-$2,500 at airport
- **Flights:** Book 2-3 months ahead; Southwest has free checked bags; Sep-Nov cheapest
- **Food:** Costco Kona membership essential; farmers markets in Hilo (Wed/Sat)
- **Rentals:** Contact property managers directly for 90-day rates; returning guest discounts 5-15%

## Scorecard

| Factor | Buy | Vacation |
|--------|-----|---------|
| Annual cost | ~$25K | ~$25K |
| Capital at risk | $450K+ in one illiquid asset | $430K+ diversified |
| Flexibility | Fixed location | Choose anywhere each year |
| Hassle | HOA, maintenance, insurance | Pack and go |
| Income potential | Possible rental income | Investment returns |
| Tax burden | Higher (non-primary rate) | Lower |
| Worst case | Special assessments, hurricane, market drop | Rental prices rise ~3%/yr |
| Best case | Appreciation + rental income | Strong market returns |

---

## Recommendation

**For 3 months/year usage, vacationing wins financially.** Annual costs are comparable, but buying ties up $430K+ in a single illiquid asset with rising carrying costs. Renting preserves capital that can generate returns roughly equal to the vacation costs themselves.

**Buying makes sense only if:**
1. They plan to eventually live there **6+ months/year** (homeowner tax rate cuts costs ~40%)
2. They want to leave the property to heirs
3. They have strong emotional attachment to a specific place
4. They can generate meaningful rental income during vacant months
